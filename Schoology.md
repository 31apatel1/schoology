# Figures of Speech:
 - Meataphor: A sentence that shows similatiry on two ideas not using like of as.
 - Simile: A statement that connects two places uing like or as.
 - Foreshadow: A phrase that relates to something that will happen later in the story.
 - Hyperbole: A literary device that's all about exaggeration for effect.
# Inventions
 - The Flying Shuttle
   - John Kay
   - 1730s
   - doubles amount of cotton cloth made in a day
   - The Spinning Jenny
 - James Hargreaves
   - 1764
   - Could spin several threads at a time, however the thread was too thin, so it broke (the thread)
 - Water Frame
   - Richard Arkwright
   - 1769
   - Steam power, rollers → to stretch thread before it was spun, tightly woven cotton fabric, durable
 - Mule
   - Samuel Orompton
   - Late 1700s
   - Steam powered, increased production of durable thread, 10 times faster than, Spinning Jenny + Water Frame = Mule
